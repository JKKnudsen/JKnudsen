TIG pedal to the Metal!

The discovery of metal-smelting is lost to time, Lead and Tin can be reduced

in an open fire. So it's possible our ancestors did it by accident a few times.

Both Tin and Lead are rather soft metals, and would never have the impact as the next

step in metallurgy.

It was likely in the reducing fires of a charcoal pottery kiln. That the first prills

of Copper was produced about 8k years ago. Unlike lead, Copper was fairly hard, and could

hold an edge. And after about 1500 years of experimentation, someone added Tin to Copper

in the right proportions to make Bronze. A metal so impactful it would be the rise and

fall of the first empires in the world.
