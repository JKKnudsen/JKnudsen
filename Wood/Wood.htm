Wood is Good!

Few materials are as versatile as wood. A soft-wood like Sitka Spruce,

with it's high strength to weight ratio, makes the highes quality soundboards.

And was the wood of choice for aircraft frames, before the advent of closed-die casting of aluminium.

On the other side you have hard-woods like oak, with a high density and hard surface it makes finest furniture.

Hard-woods is also the choise for muted and non-resonating parts of musical instruments.

For stringed necks, it's simply Maple, but for back and sides we are free to

make more of an aesthetical choice. Chestnut, Birch, Rowan and Beech are all beautyful hard-woods

with exelent wear-resistance.

And all the wood-species mentioned here, I cultivate at home and process on my sawmill.

The lumber will then air-dry, or go in a vacuum-kiln, depending on application.

I then work the stabilized wood into a variety products.With an oiled stained finish for the rough outdoors.

And a high polish, rosin based, homemade varnish, for protecting delicate musical instruments.
