Ahead of the curved Diffraction-Grating

A friend found a defective vis/uv spectroscope in the trash.

And kindly donated the grating to my prepetual poking of nature.

I will assume the reader is familliar with diffraction, and plane gratings.

The classical prism and plane grating are great for demonstrations.

But for actual measurments, additional optics are required in order to

get any usefull spectral resolution.

By using a curved diffraction grating we bring the individual spectra to a focus

dircetly, increasing our resolution and reducing noise.

Since I knew noting about the optical properties of the grating,

I made an adjustable stand for testing.

Now I havent actually done any more. The plan was to make an enclosure,

and use it with a standard 1.25" connection. But I never manage to decide

on a detector. CCD is fast, ok sensitivity, but pricey.

Photomultiplier is slow, ultra sensitive, ok price.

CMOS is fast, poor sensitivity, but cheap.

My main problem stems from indecisiveness when it comes to how it will be used.

If used with a telescope, PM-tube is the way to go. But that would be to slow

for laser-breakdown-spectroscopy. Maybe a modular solution, idk.

More will come when I stop beeing indecisive.
