Oh, It's Ion!

I think most with a high-voltage supply, has at some point made an ion-jet.

The idea is to ionize molecules of air, these are then accelerated by the electric field.

Along the journey from the electrode with sharp to smooth field-gradient.

The ion bumps into neutral gas molecules, these are then accelerated,

and beeing nutral, they are free to leave out the back.


Mine was used a 555-timer flyback circuit. It was a DC Flyback, but that does not matter.

It's the field gradient that is responcible for ionazation, anion or cathions both push the air.

Even if its AC.

You can observe the coronal discharge between the electrodes, here just a wire point,

and a thicker but smooth coil. The air leaving the back is pushing a thin strip of paper.

I also experimented with multiple electrodes.

Fun experiment, but a bit lack luster. Back then(2015) I couldn't see how to get a small,

but powerfull enough supply, to drive a plane or hovercraft.

But these days you can get some incredible small neon-transformers,

that can probably do something usefull.
