Glass is characterized as an amorphous solid. That is to say it has no crystaline lattice

structure over a long range. Others include gels and polymers. The mineral mostly

assosiated with glass is Silicon DiOxide. But in it's pure form the meltingpoint is about 1710Â°C

requiring the use of a Hydrogen-Oxygen torch. That is why most glass used today also contain

addetives, such as sodium oxide, that acts as a flux, lowering the meltingpoint by simply

dissolving the SiO2 in the flux. Other trace elements can add color, or change the mechanical

and optical properties of the glass. In the menu, you will find a compilation of physical

characteristics, for various common glass types. This is not a comprehensive lexicon, but is

intendid to serve as a general lookup for my own projects. But may serve others in
