It's crystal clear!

Early homonids did at some point realize that certain stones, did not fracture like the others.

Flint, Quartz and Obsidian would all make what we today call a Conchoidal fracture.

Leaving a sharp edge, excelent for tools and weaponry. Spuring on humanitys first Silicon revolution.

And our introduction to amorphous solids.

But it was not until mastery of ceramics that humans had their own volcanoes.

And somewhere in the middle-east about 6k years ago, someone probably made a mistake mixing glaze.

This glaze was now rich in sodium and silicate, with the sodium acting as a flux,

Lowering the melting point of the scilicate. So when the pot was fired, small translucent prills

would run of the pot. It was probably not the first time it happened, but someone got the idea,

to rework these prills in an open flame, and made the first glass beads in an early form

of what we today call lampworking.

The next development was casting of large blanks, that where then cut and ground into shape.

It was not until the Romans that the modern art of glass-blowing was invented. And large,

glass objects could be made at a fraction of the cost.
