A Stone's throw away!

Even the capuchin monkeys has seen the value in stone as a tool, and carefully selects

the finest riverstones for their hammers. These are then carried to dedicated anvil sites,

because the capuchin knows that not any old rock makes a good anvil. Don't want nut's flying everywhere!

So it should come as no supprise that the greates period of humanity's excistance, is called the "Stone Age"

The techniques developed by early humans over the first 400k years, has changed little, even to this day.

Percussion and Abrasives, is still the name of the game. But these days, there is usually a motor

driving, what used to be by hand, oxen, wind or water wheel.

Now in the last 150years or so, the efficiancy of ore processing, and the discovery of synthetic abrassives

like Silicon Carbide and Diamonds, has made high quality abrasives abundant.

But Aluminium Oxide, as in the Corundum of old, is still a popular choice for fine grinding, water-jet-cutters

and sandblasting.
